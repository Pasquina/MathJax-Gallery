Classical geometry is the study of geometric figures (points, lines, planes, circles) at rest using deductive reasoning, foundational axiomatic systems, and compass-and-straightedge constructions, famously organized in Euclid's _Elements_. It focuses on spatial relationships, congruence, and similarity, forming the basis for Euclidean geometry, which models physical space.
